About This Project

Our Mission

This project began as a personal journey to uncover and preserve the stories behind the names in our family tree. What started as a handful of census records and faded photographs has grown into a collaborative effort spanning multiple branches, generations, and continents.

We believe that understanding where we come from gives us a richer sense of who we are. Every birth date, marriage record, and migration story adds another thread to the tapestry of our shared heritage.

What You’ll Find Here

Profile Pages — Detailed genealogical profiles for individuals in the family, including vital facts, biographies, family group information (parents, spouses, children), and sourced references.

Articles — In-depth write-ups on research breakthroughs, historical context, migration patterns, and the methodology behind our findings.

Mixed-Family Examples — Real-world demonstrations of how blended families—biological parents, step-parents, multiple spouses, and adopted children—are represented in genealogical records.

Research Standards

We strive to follow the Genealogical Proof Standard established by the Board for Certification of Genealogists. Every claim is supported by at least one source citation, and conflicting evidence is noted where applicable.

Primary sources referenced include federal and state census records, vital records (birth, marriage, death certificates), church registers, cemetery records, probate files, and contemporary newspaper accounts.

Contributing

This is very much a living project. If you have corrections, additional sources, photographs, or family stories to share, we would love to hear from you.

Please reach out through our contact page or connect with us directly. Every contribution helps build a more complete and accurate family record for future generations.
